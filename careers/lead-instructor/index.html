Lead Instructor

The Lead Instructor owns instructional coherence, delivery standards, and quality across the cybersecurity and digital forensics program. This role is ideal for a senior educator-practitioner who can bridge technical credibility, learner support, and program-level leadership.

Program Context

This role supports a hybrid, artifact-based program model that combines shared foundations, specialized tracks, applied labs, and capstone work.

What You Lead

Instructional standards and program coherence.

Set expectations for teaching quality, technical rigor, learner support, assessments, and handoffs between the cybersecurity and digital forensics tracks.

What You Do

Guide delivery across instructors, TAs, and cohorts.

Lead selected concept sessions, approve instructional direction, coordinate support instructors, and make sure delivery stays aligned to program outcomes.

What Success Looks Like

Learners build real capability and partners see quality.

The program runs with clear standards, strong learner support, visible artifacts, and confidence from employers and institutional partners.

Candidate Profile

We are looking for technical depth, calm leadership, and instructional judgment.

Strong candidates can teach clearly, manage complexity, support a hybrid team, and protect quality without losing sight of learner experience.
